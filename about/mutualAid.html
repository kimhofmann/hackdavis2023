What is Mutual Aid?

Mutual Aid is a philosophy and practice based on solidarity, cooperation, and collective support. It emphasizes the idea that individuals and communities can come together to meet their needs and address social issues without relying solely on formal institutions or systems. Mutual Aid encourages reciprocal relationships where everyone contributes and benefits, recognizing that we all have strengths and resources to share. It fosters a sense of empowerment, self-reliance, and community resilience. By promoting cooperation over competition, Mutual Aid aims to create a more equitable and inclusive society where everyone's well-being is prioritized.
